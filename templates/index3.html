What Are STDs?

Sexually Transmitted Diseases (STDs) are infections passed through sexual contact, caused by bacteria, viruses, or parasites. Early awareness and testing are key to treatment and prevention.





Common STDs Explained

  • Chlamydia is one of the most common STDs caused by the bacterium Chlamydia trachomatis. It often presents no symptoms, especially in women, which makes regular screening crucial. When symptoms occur, they may include pain during urination, unusual genital discharge, and discomfort during intercourse. If left untreated, chlamydia can cause serious complications such as pelvic inflammatory disease (PID), infertility, and ectopic pregnancy.

  • Gonorrhea is a bacterial STD caused by Neisseria gonorrhoeae. Like chlamydia, it often shows no symptoms, particularly in women. When symptoms do appear, they may include painful urination, increased vaginal or penile discharge, and pain or swelling in the testicles. If untreated, gonorrhea can lead to severe reproductive and systemic health problems such as PID, infertility, and joint infections.

  • HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) attacks the body’s immune system, specifically the CD4 cells (T cells). If not treated, it can lead to AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome). There is no cure for HIV, but antiretroviral therapy (ART) can control the virus and allow individuals to live long, healthy lives.

  • Genital herpes is caused by the herpes simplex virus (HSV-1 or HSV-2). It is a lifelong infection that remains dormant in the body and can cause recurrent outbreaks. Symptoms include painful blisters or sores in the genital area, itching, and flu-like symptoms during outbreaks. It can also be spread even when there are no visible symptoms (this is called asymptomatic shedding), but outbreaks carry the highest risk.

  • Syphilis is a bacterial infection caused by Treponema pallidum. It progresses through four stages: primary, secondary, latent, and tertiary. Early symptoms may include painless sores or ulcers, followed by rashes and flu-like symptoms. If untreated, it can lead to serious health complications affecting the heart, brain, and other organs.

  • HPV is the most common sexually transmitted infection caused by a group of related viruses. It can cause genital warts and some strains are associated with cancer, including cervical, anal, and throat cancer. The HPV vaccine is the most effective way to prevent infection with the most harmful types of HPV.

  • Trichomoniasis is a parasitic infection caused by Trichomonas vaginalis. It is one of the most common STIs worldwide. Trichomoniasis often presents no symptoms but can cause discomfort and irritation, particularly in women. It can be easily treated with antibiotics. Trichomoniasis can still spread through contact with infected genital areas even without ejaculation, so consistent use is key.

How to Stay Protected

  • Always use condoms or dental dams during sexual activity.
  • Get vaccinated against HPV and hepatitis B.
  • Regular check-ups, even when you feel fine.
  • Open communication with partners about sexual health.
  • Never share needles or unsterile instruments.
  • Regular Screening like Pap smears for women.
  • Avoid sharing Sexual Toys.




Symptoms

  • Chlamydia : Abnormal discharge from the penis or vagina
  • Gonorrhea : Increased discharge from the penis or vagina
  • HIV/AIDS : Swollen lymph nodes Frequent infections
  • Genital Herpes : Painful blisters or sores in the genital or anal area
  • Syphilis : Painless sores (chancres) at the site of infection
  • HPV : Often asymptomatic; most people do not show symptoms
  • Trichomoniasis : Itching, burning, or redness in the genital area
